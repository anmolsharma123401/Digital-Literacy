**Cybercrime Type: Phishing**

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy source. It commonly occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that look legitimate.

A typical phishing attack happens step-by-step like this: First, the attacker creates a fake message that appears to come from a bank, social media platform, or trusted company. Next, the message urges the victim to take immediate action, such as “verify your account” or “reset your password.” It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look real. When the victim enters their details, the attacker captures the information. Finally, the attacker uses this data to access accounts or commit financial fraud.

Phishing usually targets people who are less aware of digital security practices, such as students, elderly users, or individuals who frequently use online banking and shopping. However, even experienced users can fall victim if the message looks convincing.

The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money, have their personal data stolen, or face identity theft. In some cases, their accounts may be misused for further scams. This not only causes financial loss but also emotional stress and loss of trust in digital platforms. Therefore, awareness and caution are essential to prevent such attacks.
